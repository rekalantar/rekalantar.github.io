About me

I am currently a final year PhD student at the Institue of Cancer Research. My research involves developing artificial intelligence (AI)-based models for automatic segmentation and quantitative evaluation of pelvic cancers. My key research topics include unsupervised image synthesis, domain adaptation, semantic knowledge transfer and AI explainability. Previously, I obtained my MRes degree from Imperial College London. in medical robotics and image-guided interventions, and MEng/BEng degree from the University of Leeds. Furthermore, I completed two erasmus programmes at Simon Fraser University in Canada and the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology in Hong Kong.


I have a strong interest in technology and improving healthcare. Throughout my life, I have taken on many challenges that have shaped me to the versatile and intelectualy-curious person that I am today. Along my educational experiences abroad, I was my course representative and led my group team project for degree at Imperial College London. I also have teaching experience where I designed and taught engieering mathematics during my A-levels.
