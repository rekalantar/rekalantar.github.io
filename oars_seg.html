Domain Aaptive Organs-at-Risk (OARs) Segmentation for Radiotherapy Planning

Why is domain adaptation important in medical imaging?

Over the past decade, numerous deep learning applications and architectures have been proposed to utilize the impressive ability of machines in learning complex features from large pools of data. This is particularly appealing in computer vision applications where applications are trained on natural images.

In the medical imaging world, access to large datasets is limited due to inter-vendor and image acquisition variabilities, patient data confidentiality and the significant time required by expert clinicians to delineate regions of interest (ROIs). Even though some research studies have reported that using transfer learning from large pre-trained networks (e.g. VGG-16, VGG-19), they still fail to learn anatomical semantic knowledge for cross-domain segmentation tasks.

One potential solution to tackle the lack of high-quality and annotated data in medical imaging is domain adaptation. In essence, domain adaptation denotes transfer of feature-level knowledge from one domain to another. This can be promising in cancer radiotherapy planning where anatomical segmentation knowledge from CT scans can be transferred to MRI for MR-guided/MR-only treatment systems (e.g. MR-Linac).
